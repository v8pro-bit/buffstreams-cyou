DMCA Policy - BuffStreams

BuffStreams (https://buffstreams.cyou) respects the intellectual property rights of others and operates in compliance with the Digital Millennium Copyright Act (DMCA). This page outlines our policy regarding copyright infringement claims and the process for submitting a DMCA takedown request.

Content Policy

BuffStreams does not host, store, or upload any copyrighted content on its servers. All streaming links found on our website are sourced from publicly available third-party websites. We do not claim ownership of any media content provided by these external sources.

Reporting Copyright Infringement

If you believe that your copyrighted work has been linked or embedded on our website without authorization, you may submit a DMCA takedown request. Please include the following details:

  • Your Contact Information: Name, email address, and physical address.
  • Identification of the Copyrighted Work: A description of the copyrighted material that you claim has been infringed.
  • Infringing URL(s): The exact URL(s) of the allegedly infringing content on BuffStreams.
  • Proof of Ownership: Documentation or links proving that you own the rights to the content.
  • A Statement of Good Faith: A declaration that you have a good faith belief that the use of the material is unauthorized.
  • A Statement of Accuracy: A declaration, under penalty of perjury, that the information in your notice is accurate and that you are the copyright owner or authorized to act on behalf of the owner.
  • Your Signature: A physical or electronic signature of the copyright owner or authorized representative.

Where to Send DMCA Notices

Please submit all DMCA takedown notices via email to: buffstreams.cyou@gmail.com

Response to DMCA Complaints

Upon receiving a valid DMCA complaint, we will:

  • Review the request and verify its authenticity.
  • Remove or disable access to the allegedly infringing content if the complaint is valid.
  • Notify the user responsible for the content, if applicable.

Counter-Notification Process

If you believe that your content was removed in error, you may submit a counter-notification. Your counter-notice must include:

  • Your Contact Information: Name, email address, and physical address.
  • Identification of the Removed Content: The specific URLs of the content that was removed.
  • A Statement of Good Faith: A declaration that you have a good faith belief that the content was removed due to a mistake or misidentification.
  • A Statement of Consent: A declaration consenting to the jurisdiction of your local court or any applicable authority.
  • Your Signature: A physical or electronic signature.

Important Notice

We only process valid DMCA requests. Misuse of the DMCA process may result in legal consequences.

We recommend contacting the original hosting site directly, as we do not control or host any of the content ourselves.
